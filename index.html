GEU WORKS WITH DONORS AND IMPLEMENTING AGENCIES TO DELIVER BETTER RESULTS FOR ADOLESCENT GIRLS LIVING IN POVERTY.

Managed by Mercy Corps, Girl Effect University (GEU) is a mobile learning platform and behavior change catalyst that delivers training, tools, inspiration and networks to help unleash the potential of 250 million girls living in poverty.

TRAINING

GEU workshops are highly experiential, equipping development professionals with the knowledge, tools and hands-on practice to deliver more effective programming for adolescent girls.

COMMUNITY

The GEU alumni network is a platform for exchanging knowledge, experience and resources with a global community of girl-centered development practitioners.

ADVISING

GEU technical advisors work with implementing agencies to identify areas in which technical assistance, training and coaching can have the biggest impact for girls. Advisors review and evaluate existing programs, scope new program opportunities and advise on wider strategies and operational frameworks for effective and targeted programming to better meet the needs of adolescent girls.

COACHING

GEU coaches provide sustained guidance and support to strengthen human and institutional capacity, empowering staff to confidently practice girl-centered design so that implementation is possible and effective. Coaches are knowledgeable experts and creative problems solvers who collaborate with staff to think and work through challenges and help develop clear, straightforward ideas for implementing effective activities, methods and interventions for girls.

OUR METHODOLOGY

GEU builds the ability for development professionals to place adolescent girls at the center of program design and implementation. Practitioners learn how to:

  • Meet the unique needs of girls by intentionally defining which girls, why those girls, and how to best engage them in all aspects of programming.
  • Position girls as the experts of their situations and directly engage with girls throughout the program lifecycle.
  • Provide safe spaces for girls to make connections, find a mentor, gain knowledge skills, learn their rights and have fun.
  • Disaggregate data in all monitoring, evaluation and research activities to find and reach the most vulnerable girls.
